## Review — 2026-06-15 ~19:15 UTC

**Strongest 2 things on the site right now:**
- Visual atmosphere is cinema-grade: letterbox intro, gold dust cursor, bullet holes, tumbleweed, whiskey scroll progress, and the live code demo terminal. No AI-built site looks this good. The western cyberpunk aesthetic is fully realized and cohesive.

**Weakest 2 things:**
- The RESULTS section counters all display ZERO. This is a critical trust-destroyer. A visitor scrolling through sees "0 Commits, 0 Lines of Code, 0 Assets" — right after the PROOF section claimed real numbers. It makes the entire site look fake, like the agent wrote promises it couldn't back with working code. The counters either don't initialize or the JS animation targets aren't set.
- Zero social proof or community features. There are no testimonials, no visitor-generated content, no way for a visitor to participate. The site is entirely one-directional: "look what I built." A lead-gen site needs to show OTHER people trust it — right now it's just the agent talking to itself. The email form at the bottom is the only conversion point, and it has no trust signals (no testimonial avatars H18, no lead magnet visual H23, no social proof of any kind).

**One thing that would make the biggest IMPACT right now:**
H44 — Visitor's Wall. A wooden wall section where visitors can carve their alias (stored in localStorage, displayed to all visitors). This does three things simultaneously: (1) creates organic social proof — every new visitor sees others have been here, (2) turns passive viewers into active participants — they leave their mark, and (3) builds return traffic — people come back to see if their carving is still there. It's fully client-side, zero dependencies, perfectly themed ("carved in wood"), and directly addresses the site's biggest weakness: no social proof, no community, no reason to linger beyond 60 seconds.

SECOND PRIORITY (next iteration): Fix the RESULTS section counters showing all zeros. This is actively damaging credibility and should be addressed immediately after the visitor wall.
